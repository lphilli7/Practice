    Project Overview

    The real estate market is an ever-present feature of our lives, and beneath the surface is a rich source of economic factors that make it tick. It is, therefore, a highly important topic, which is interwoven with data elements, that affects all people from the CEO of a Fortune 500 company to your average home-owner. It is with this knowledge that we explore the United States Real Estate Market wiht the hopes of bringing insight into how the market has functioned in the past and continues through the present and into the future.

    Exploration Questions:

    1. Does GDP effect the real estate market?
    2. How do home prices change with inflation?
    3. Is unemployment correlated with the performance of the real estate market?
    4. What geographies have been the most affected by fluctations in market factors?
    5. What periods of time have seen the most abnormalities?
    6. How can this information empower homeowners and prospective homeowners?

    Real Estate Basics

    • Consumer Price Index (CPI): A measure that examines the weighted average of prices of a basket of consumer goods and services, such as transportation, food, and medical care.
    • Federal Funds Rate (Fed Funds): The interest rate at which depository institutions lend reserve balances to other banks overnight on an uncollateralized basis, set by the Federal Reserve.
    • Gross Domestic Product (GDP): The total monetary value of all finished goods and services produced within a country's borders in a specific time period.
    • Home Price Index (HPI): A measure that tracks changes in the sales prices of homes over time within a specific region or market.
    • 30-Year Mortgage Rate: The interest rate charged on a conventional 30-year fixed-rate mortgage, typically used to finance the purchase of a home.
    • Population Growth Rate: The rate at which the population of a particular area grows in a given period, usually expressed as a percentage.
    • Unemployment Rate: The percentage of the total labor force that is unemployed and actively seeking employment during a specific period.
